Google UX Design course project

A mobile app for a casual Japanese restaurant. The target users are those who eat outside regularly and look for an efficient way to book a table for meal because of busy working life.

UX design is an iteration task, so this project will be updated in future.

Persona: Allen

Problem statement:
Allen is a System Analyst and a father of a kid who needs a restaurant app providing efficient way for booking a table because he is busy for work in working hours and he have to take care his kid in spare time.

User journey map

Mapping Allen's user journey revealed how helpful it would be for users to have access to a dedicated Japanese restaurant app.

Paper wireframes

Drafts of iterations of each screen of the mobile app on paper ensured that the elements that made it to digital wireframes would be appropriate to address user pain points.

For the home screen, I arranged the reservation buttons appeared in different locations to facilitate the visitors for booking.

Digital wireframes

As the initial design phase continued, I made sure to base screen designs on feedback and findings from the user research.

Digital wireframes

A clear feedback for completion of process could provide visitors a clear recognition for the success of the procedure.

Low-fidelity prototype

The low-fidelity prototype connected the primary user flow of making a reservation, so the prototype could be used in a usability study with users.

High-fidelity prototype

The high-fidelity prototype shows the product in its closest resemblance to the final design in terms of details and functionality.

Logo concept

Katei No Aji means taste of home and this is what we want our customers to savour in our restaurant.

This is why our logo consists of an image of house, a Japan flag, and a pair of chopsticks which are going to pick up a sushi roll.
